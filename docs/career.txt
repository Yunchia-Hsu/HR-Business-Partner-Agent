ICEYE Career Development and Performance Guide
Last updated: January 2026

---

PERFORMANCE REVIEW CYCLE

ICEYE runs a bi-annual performance review cycle in June and December.
The June review focuses on mid-year progress and goal alignment.
The December review is comprehensive and includes promotion and compensation decisions.
Employees set their goals at the beginning of each year in collaboration with their manager.
Goals should follow the SMART framework: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound.
Self-assessment forms must be submitted at least one week before the review meeting.

---

CAREER LEVELS

ICEYE uses a transparent career framework with defined levels for each function.
Engineering levels: Associate, Engineer, Senior Engineer, Staff Engineer, Principal Engineer.
Each level has a defined set of competencies covering technical skills, impact, and collaboration.
The career framework document is available on the HR portal at hr.iceye.fi/careers.
Employees are encouraged to discuss their current level and growth areas with their manager.

---

PROMOTION PROCESS

Promotions are considered during the December performance review.
To be eligible for promotion, an employee must have been performing consistently at the next level for at least 6 months.
The direct manager initiates a promotion case and submits it to the HR committee.
Promotion decisions are made by the HR committee in consultation with senior leadership.
Employees will receive written feedback regardless of whether the promotion is approved.
If a promotion is not approved, the manager will provide a clear development plan with specific milestones.

HOW TO HAVE A CAREER DEVELOPMENT CONVERSATION WITH YOUR MANAGER

Career development conversations should happen at least once per quarter.
Prepare by reflecting on: what you have achieved, what you want to grow in, and what support you need.
Useful questions to bring to your manager:
- "What are the most important skills I should develop to reach the next level?"
- "Where do you see my biggest opportunities for growth right now?"
- "Are there projects or responsibilities I could take on to stretch my skills?"
- "How does my work compare to the expectations for the next level?"
Be specific about your ambitions. Saying "I want to grow" is less effective than "I want to lead a project end-to-end within the next 6 months."

---

HOW TO PREPARE FOR A PERFORMANCE CONVERSATION

Before the meeting, review your goals and gather concrete examples of your contributions.
Use the STAR method to describe your achievements: Situation, Task, Action, Result.
Be honest about areas where you fell short and come with ideas on how to improve.
If you disagree with feedback, listen fully before responding. Ask clarifying questions.
Agree on next steps and write them down. Follow up in writing after the meeting.

---

MENTORING AND COACHING

ICEYE offers a voluntary internal mentoring programme matching employees across departments.
Mentoring pairs meet at least once a month for 6 months.
To join the mentoring programme, apply through the HR portal.
External coaching is available for senior employees upon request and manager approval.

---

INTERNAL MOBILITY

Employees who have been in their current role for at least 12 months may apply for internal transfers.
Open internal positions are posted on the HR portal before being listed externally.
Interested employees should speak with their current manager before applying.
Internal candidates receive priority consideration for suitable roles.

---

FEEDBACK CULTURE

ICEYE encourages a continuous feedback culture. You do not need to wait for a review cycle.
Give feedback promptly, specifically, and constructively.
Use the model: observation + impact + suggestion.
Example: "When you shared the project update without the key metrics (observation), the stakeholders could not assess progress (impact). Next time, could you include the dashboard link? (suggestion)"
If you are uncomfortable giving feedback directly, you can request a facilitated conversation through HR.
